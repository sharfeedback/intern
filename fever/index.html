Normal Temperature

  • Normal body temperature=97.7-99.5 °F(36.5-37.5°C)
  • Normal children temperature=97.4-100 °F(36.3-37.7°C)

Fever Temperature

  • Axillary temperature- >99.5-99.7°F
  • Oral temperature- >99.5-99.9°F(Oral temperature is 0.6°C /1°F higher than temperature recorded in the axilla.)
  • Rectal temperature- >100.4 °F(Rectal temperature is 0.6°C /1°F higher than oral temperature.)
  • Tymapanic temperature- >100.4°F

Treatment

  • ANTIPYRETICS (Meftal may be given Stat for high fever)

  • Tepid sponging with luke warm water

  • Antibiotics if infection suspected

    Diet

  • Soft foods

  • Water 2-3 hr once

  • Fruits

  • Avoid-Junk,oil and fried foods

General Treatment in Adults

  • TAB.PARACETAMOL
  • Tepid sponging Cough/fever -TAB.CEFADROXIL 500mg BD x 5 days

General Treatment in Children 4-6 years old

  • SYP.PARACETAMOL/CROCIN 2 tsp
  • Tepid sponging
  • High/repeated fever-SYP.AMOXYCILLIN/WYMOX 2tsp TDS

General Treatment in Infants upto 1 year old

  • SYP.PARACETAMOL/CROCIN 1-2ml TDS
  • Tepid sponging
  • High/repeated fever-SYP.AMOXYCILLIN/WYMOX 2ml TDS

Types-

  • Continous fever(The temperature remains elevated above normal without touching the baseline and the fluctuation does not exceed 0.6°C /1°F diurnal variation)
    • Pneumonia
    • Meningitis
    • UTI
    • Brucella
  • Intermittent fever(The elevated temperature touches the baseline in between. In hectic or septic type of intermittent fever, the diurnal variation is extremely large, as occurs in septicaemia. Quotidian fever is a hectic fever occurring daily)
  • Remittent Fever (The temperature fluctuation exceeds 0.6°C (1°F), but without touching the baseline)
    • TB
    • Infective endocarditis
    • Many viral and bacterial infections
  • Relapsing Fever(Febrile episodes are separated by normal temperature for more than one day)
    • Malaria
    • Borrelia
    • TB
    • Lymphoma
  • Step ladder fever(a type of sustained fever where the temperature rises gradually to a higher level with every spike)
    • Typhoid
  • Night sweats
    • TB
